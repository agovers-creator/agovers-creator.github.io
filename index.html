Catalytic Profiling of Putative Oleate Hydratases

Oleate hydratases (Ohys) are industrially promising enzymes used in the production of polymers and cosmetic emulsifiers, but their limited thermostability makes them diﬃcult to deploy at scale. This project set out to find a more robust alternative through genome mining: systematically searching thermophilic bacterial genomes for novel enzyme variants. I screened ten putative oleate hydratase candidates, managed their soluble expression in E. coli, and performed functional and stability characterisation, narrowing ten candidates down to one active thermophilic hit.

Characterisation of Enzymatic Reductive Amination

Performed laboratory research on the reductive aminase RytRedAm to evaluate and optimise enzymatic reductive amination reactions relevant to biocatalysis and pharmaceutical synthesis. Responsibilities included recombinant protein expression in Escherichia coli, experimental design, reaction optimisation, and analytical assessment of substrate conversion under varying reaction conditions. Identified optimal operational parameters for enzyme activity and evaluated substrate specificity across multiple aldehydes and ketones. Findings supported further development of enzyme stability and catalytic eﬃciency for industrial bioprocess applications.
